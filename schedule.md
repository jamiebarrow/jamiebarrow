# Schedule

I will try to be aware and concious of the [buckets of time](./time-buckets.md) in my life,
and have the below schedule as a goal in order to live a balanced, purposeful and happy life.

This is not set in stone, and with self-compassion I will allow myself to not be perfect,
as perfectionism is often not realistic.

### Daily Routine

Being healthy is important to feeling relaxed, focused, and having a stable mood.
This includes regular sleep patterns, exercise and healthy eating habits.

I am employed on a full-time basis, 5 days a week, 8 hours a day, with at least a 30 minute lunch break,
and in order to have a more calm and less busy day, I may take an hour lunch break.

To start my day I wake up when my alarm goes off at 7am, get up immediately no matter how tired I am,
no matter what the weather is, and I go for a short walk around the block to get my body started.
After my walk I will take a shower to feel refreshed for the day.
Breakfast will be easy, quick to prepare (or prepared the night before), and with a cup of coffee.

My current job involves quite a lot of meetings.
Depending on the day there may be no set time to block out for deeply focused work.
Collaboration with the team(s) also means I cannot be isolated entirely to focus on specific tasks.
I would like to change this over time to rather be:
- mornings for deep work
- afternoons for meetings and team ceremonies and collaboration

After work I will first spend some time alone to wind down and clear my thoughts for the evening routine.

The evening is focused around family and quality time, beginning with preparing dinner together.
We will enjoy dinner together without distractions - no cellphones.
After dinner is time for some quick chores like washing dishes and tidying up.
However, its most important that after dinner time is family and quality time spent together.

At the end of the evening is personal time, where I can spend 2 hours however I wish to spend it.
Usually leisure time, time to work on personal projects and learning, or maybe chores.

The end of the night starts with unwinding from the day, disconnecting from technology,
preparing for bed and having a relaxing sleep.


### Weekend Routine

The weekend allows time to focus on family, friends, hobbies and to take a break from work.
To keep to a routine, the schedule should be as close to the weekly schedule as possible.

Family & Friends:
- weekly chores
- socializing
- quality time with significant other

Hobbies:
- non-work related computer stuff
- reading
- cycling
- playing games
- art
